Featured Tutorial

Basic Square Base Technique

Beginner
1

Prepare Your Materials

Select 8-10 bamboo strips of equal width (approx. 5mm). Soak in water for 30 minutes to increase flexibility.

2

Create Your Base

Arrange 4 strips horizontally and 4 strips vertically to form a square grid pattern.

3

Begin Weaving

Start from the center, weaving each strip over and under in an alternating pattern.

Historical Context

Origins & Evolution

Bamboo weaving techniques date back over 7,000 years in China, originating during the Neolithic period. Archaeological findings from the Hemudu culture (5000-4500 BCE) include bamboo baskets with sophisticated weaving patterns.

Master Craftsmen Tips

Master Chen Jianguo

National Intangible Cultural Heritage Inheritor

"Always soak your bamboo strips until they are completely pliable. If they resist bending, they need more time in water."

"The thickness of your strips should be consistent throughout. Take time to prepare your materials properly before beginning to weave."

"When creating a tight weave, use a small wooden tool to gently push strips together rather than pulling with force, which can cause breakage."

Bamboo Selection

For beginners, 2-3 year old Moso bamboo (Phyllostachys edulis) is recommended for its flexibility and durability. Look for straight culms with consistent coloring.
